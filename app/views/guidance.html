Introduction to requesting data

The Data Access Request Service (DARS) allows clinicians, researchers and commissioners to make an application for data to help improve NHS services.

The data set catalogue (opens in a new window) provides information on data sets readily available to request through DARS. Data sets may be provided in the Trusted Research Environment (TRE) (opens in a new window) or as an extract or through the DigiTrials Service (opens in a new window)

The metadata dashboard (opens in a new window) provides metadata on publicly available fields for available data sets.

To obtain the data, you must apply for a Data Sharing Agreement (DSA).

Once you begin making an application, you will be assigned a NIC number. Quoting this NIC number each time you communicate with the Data Applications Team will help us quickly find your application details. Where you request more of the same data, select to retain the data you have, cancel your DSA or make a change to your existing DSA, your NIC version number will change.

A DSA is an agreement between you and NHS England that sets out:

  • the purpose of data-sharing
  • what happens to the data at each stage
  • how you can comply with the ‘standards’ to enable the sharing of data
  • roles and responsibilities of all of those involved in data sharing

Data cannot be shared, and the application for a DSA will not be accepted unless the Controller organisation has a valid Data Sharing Framework Contract (DSFC). You can still start an application for a DSA while you are waiting for your DSFC.

If you are unsure whether the listed Controller organisation has a valid DSFC, contact us on 0300 303 5678, or email enquiries@nhsdigital.nhs.uk

The DSFC sets out:

  • the responsibilities of the recipient organisation
  • restrictions on data use
  • the security to be provided by the recipient
  • how to keep information confidential
  • how to meet the requirements of the Data Protection Act
  • how to ensure the destruction of information at the end of a contract

Users of the DARS Online system should use this guidance to help them when:

  • completing a new application for data
  • making a request for more data
  • making a request to retain data
  • closing a DSA
  • making a change to a DSA
  • applying for a DSFC
  • approving a DSFC
  • renewing a DSFC

NHS England has produced separate guidance on the information we expect to see in the various sections of a DARS application, the separate sets of guidance are known as ‘Standards’. For each ‘Standard’ there is guidance that covers important items to consider when making an application. You can find links to the expected standards to the right of this page under ‘Detailed Guidance’.

Referring to the Standards (opens in a new window) and the below ‘General tips for all DSA applications’, will help you submit a successful application.

General tips for all DSA applications

Help us understand how you will use the data and what benefits data processing will bring

When applying, you should write so that it is easily understood and covers all the planned uses of the data. Be specific, clear, and unambiguous, and explain which organisations are involved, to include:

  • who the Controller (opens in a new window) is and whether they are sole or joint
  • who the Processor is (opens in a new window)
  • whether any other organisations are involved in the wider project (but not processing the data), and if so, the nature of their role
  • whether there are any Funders, Sponsors or Commissioners involved, and if so, the nature of their role

If your purpose is commercial, make sure you clearly explain what you do commercially, why you do it, who your customers are, and how that relates to your application. You must tell us how data processing will benefit the health and social system in England and Wales. The information you need to provide will depend on the degree of commercial involvement.

Make sure you cover all specific uses. If others are using NHS England's data, or accessing the outputs of your work, make clear exactly what data they are accessing (such as record-level, or aggregate).

NHS England publishes a Data Uses Register (opens in a new window) which includes approved purposes for previous data releases. You can use this register to see what information others have provided in their applications, and where appropriate, use similar wording.

Your finalised agreement text will be published on NHS England's Data Uses Register. Ensure it is written so that it can be easily understood. Supporting documents cannot be viewed on the Data Uses Register, so relevant information you provide within the supporting documentation section of your application should be reproduced in the agreement text. You should explain acronyms and make clear which organisation you are referring to.

Information you will need to provide within the DSA application

A DSA application is made through the DARS Online system (opens in a new window).. The application process requires information about the organisations involved such as:

  • the Controller (opens in a new window)
  • the Processor (opens in a new window)
  • processing and storage locations
  • other organisations involved

We also need information that supports why you are applying for the data, such as:

  • data request reason and project name
  • objectives and processing activities
  • outputs and benefits
  • commercial purposes and sub-licensing

And information about the:

  • data
  • participants

For more information, refer to the Standards of information expected in a data access application (opens in a new window) Standards can also found as individual links under ‘Detailed guidance’ to the right of this page.

Evidence to support the legal basis or bases

Parts of your application may require you to provide evidence that supports your legal basis.

Supporting documentation should be in PDF format, with a maximum size limit of 10 MB and must not include personal information.

If you are using s251 as a legal basis for identifiable data, you should ensure that you provide the latest application, approval, and subsequent annual review. If you are using patient consent as the legal basis, you will need to provide all templated versions of consent forms and PISs, including information that tells us how many participants consented to each templated version, without sharing their personal information. If this evidence is not provided when submitting the application, it may not be accepted.

When providing ethics approvals as a supporting document, you should provide information on amendments, such as changes to consent materials, protocol, lead investigators, or purpose.

The supporting documents we require will vary depending on the type of application you are making.

The legal basis that allows NHS England to collect and disseminate data, and applicants to process data

Ensuring that there is a legal basis for NHS England to provide the data is essential. It is the Controller's responsibility to ensure there is a legal basis under the UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR) and Common Law Duty of Confidentiality (opens in a new window). If you require external approvals, for example, a Section 251 (opens in a new window), ensure you have these before submitting an application. The Confidentiality Advisory Group (CAG) (opens in a new window) provide approval under s251.

NHS England is happy to discuss external approvals before an application is made. We welcome early sight of consent form proposals before research begins so that we can provide you with feedback before recruiting Participants. We can also help you understand if using an existing Participant group is acceptable. Email enquiries@nhsdigital.nhs.uk

If the data you wish to receive is pseudonymised, anonymised or anonymous (and no identifiable data is flowing to enable that release), then the legal basis is typically the Health and Social Care Act 2012.

If you wish to receive or provide identifiable data, then the legal basis is typically s251 (opens in a new window) or consent. Learn more about what legal basis (opens in a new window) or consent. Learn more about what legal basis (opens in a new window) will apply.

If you rely on consent from research participants to access data from NHS England, NHS England requires information contained within the PIS to be aligned with the consent forms.

If you are providing NHS England with a Participant group, you should (within the processing section of your application) indicate whether recruitment of Participants has started and whether it is complete. Where possible, you should also provide the estimated target or actual sample size of the Participant group.

You can view the ‘data items and data flow diagram’ within the pre-application checklist (opens in a new window) for guidance on which identifiers NHS England can use to link the Participant group to their health records. You must list the data items that you are supplying to NHS England in the consent form, s251 application, or PIS.

Recruiting participants to your study can be complex. NHS England encourages you to contact us prior to recruiting Participants.

In rare circumstances, there may be an alternative legal basis that applies to your request for data. If you think an alternative legal basis applies, email enquiries@nhsdigital.nhs.uk to find out.

National data opt-out (opens in a new window) allows people to opt-out of their personal data being used for purposes beyond their direct care.

Purpose of the data

Data provided to you can be used only for the purposes stated in this section. You should provide enough information to tell us about its purpose, while avoiding background information that does not help in this justification. Learn more about what data (opens in a new window) you are requesting and the charges (opens in a new window) that will apply.

NHS England will publish the information in this section within the Data Uses Register (opens in a new window).

Avoid using references to the first person, such as “we, “us”, “I”, and referencing the names of individuals. You should use third-person language where possible, for example, “he”, “she”, “it”, and “they”.

You should:

  • use the word "only" to help you describe its purpose, for example, "this data will only be used to produce..."
  • state (where appropriate) what the data will not be used for, for example, “not used for commercial purposes”, “not provided in record-level form to any third party”, “not used for direct marketing”
  • write with the assumption that your application will be viewed by the public and therefore written in simple, easy-to-understand language
  • be specific about the size of any customer base, if you have both NHS and commercial sector customers, ensure that you state the relative split between those customer segments

You should make clear:

  • the objectives for processing (specific purpose)
  • what organisations will be using the data
  • what the expected outputs are
  • whether the outputs are in record-level form, presenting unique, non-aggregated data that relate to a single identifiable individual, provider or hospital
  • the benefits to the health and social care system, and when they will be delivered
  • whether the application is in any way commercial and if so, how?

Applications (except for anonymised tabulations) which are solely for commercial purposes and do not demonstrate benefit to the health and social care system will not be approved.

Independent Group Advising on the Release of Data (IGARD)

IGARD is an advisory body to the NHS England board.

IGARD has two purposes:

  • to make general recommendations or observations to NHS England
  • to scrutinise and advise NHS England on the appropriateness of requests for dissemination of confidential information

When making an application for a DSA, there are several application pathways that could be relevant to you depending on the answers you provide to questions within the service.

The IGARD pathway will apply to most new DSA applications, and to some existing DSAs where you request changes to the information you have provided about:

  • the organisations involved
  • why you are applying for data
  • data and participants

Where your application takes the IGARD pathway, IGARD will independently assess your application for data.

For PhD or research projects typically delivering dissertations, peer-reviewed journal publications or both, see IGARD guidance (opens in a new window).

You can find out more about IGARD and what they do (opens in a new window) and if you have any further questions about IGARD, email IGARD@nhs.net

Data Sharing Framework Contracts (DSFCs)

The DSFC is an over-arching document that specifies the basis for data to be shared and outlines the terms and conditions of how data must be managed once it has been released to the Controller.

While you may start and save an application for data before you have a DSFC, you will need to have a DSFC before your application is accepted and data is released.

All organisations that request data from NHS England are required to have a DSFC. A DSFC ensures that data-sharing controls are in place, and grants NHS England the right to carry out audits to check that data is processed in line with the terms of the DSFC and DSA.

You can use DARS Online to apply for, approve, or renew a DSFC. Anyone can apply for a DSFC on behalf of an organisation, providing they have access to DARS Online.

The person that approves the DSFC is usually a senior staff member such as a Senior Information Risk Officer (SIRO) or equivalent.

If you have questions about your DSFC or need to make changes to it, email data.applications@nhsdigital.nhs.uk

Additional information

Further guidance on completing an application is available from NHS England on 0300 303 5678, or you can email data.applications@nhsdigital.nhs.uk quoting ‘DARS application’ in the subject field.

If your enquiry refers to an existing or previous application, quote your existing reference number (for example, DARS-NIC-999999-AAAAA) in the subject field.

The Standards of information expected in a data access application (opens in a new window) will support you in completing the various stages of an application for a DSA.

After you’ve submitted an application, your application will be reviewed, and the team will work with you to collect any missing details.

If you would like to withdraw or close a DSA, email enquiries@nhsdigital.nhs.uk quoting either ‘Withdraw agreement’ or ‘Close agreement’ along with your reference number in the subject field.
